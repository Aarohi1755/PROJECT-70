Campus Life

aravali hall

The school has a huge Multi-Purpose Hall – The Aravali Hall with a seating capacity of 300-400 people. The hall is used for Inter-School programmes and school functions. It also doubles up as an indoor games hall.

school library

All classes are provided with at least one library period per week. There are more than 20,000 books on various topics , subjects, the latest fiction, classics and encyclopedias.

audio visual room

Students are also encouraged to make use of this room for presentations by them for different projects. The air-conditioned AV Room facilitates workshop for students and teachers at regular intervals.

    Eco-Fest 2021

Eco-Fest 2021

This World Environment Day (June5.2021), Bhumi an NGO in association with Young World and The Hindu organised Eco-Fest, a two days Eco-Friendly Summer Camp. The camp offered a plethora of events and contests to pick from. Schools from all over the country participated for these online events. Our school students actively participated in the various competitions. In a painting competition on the topic ‘Save Oceans’. Shubham Mishra of class VII A secured third position. The painting beautifully depicts as to how our carelessness have turned majestic Ocean to trash.

what's new

  • INTERNATIONAL YOGA DAY CELEBRATION- JUNIOR SCHOOL


    Yoga takes you into the present moment. The only place where life exists.” - Unknown The Junior School celebrated the International Yoga Day with great gusto on 21 June 2021. The students of all classes UKG-V participated in a special virtual yoga session with their class teachers early morning, much before their online classes. Together they did breathing exercises, neck exercises and some simple asanas for general well being. Yoga, as is a known fact, not only improves overall fitness but also helps to retain body flexibility, increases immunity, keeps the heart, respiratory and digestive systems fit, improves body posture and increases attention span and concentration. The teachers discussed the benefits of yoga with the children and encouraged them to make it an integral part of their life style. Children promised to practice yoga regularly and impressed everyone with their yoga postures.




  • WORKSHOP ON VEDIC MATHS


    A workshop titled ‘Back to the Future’ was organised by Swiflearn in collaboration with NIE, The Times of India on 12 June 2021 on Zoom for the students of classes IV - VIII. The one-hour long session was led by Ms Harneet Kaur Gujral, B.A. Mathematics Honours, Mathematics Subject Matter Expert. The session aimed at boosting mathematical powers with Vedic Maths. It addressed the phobia that children have for maths encouraging them to do quick mental calculations in a game format unfolding the magic of Vedic maths. The children learnt as they played. The energy during the entire session was palpable as the children vied with each other to be the first to solve questions or post queries. They learnt quick rules to add, subtract and multiply big figures. All in all it was a fruitful, interactive session.
